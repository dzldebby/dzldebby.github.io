QUANTIFYING SOCIAL MEDIA REACTIONS: YOUNG ESOMAR SOCIETY AWARD WINNER

  • Role

    Lead Researcher

  • Organization

    ESOMAR Congress 2019

  • Date

    2019

  • Tools

    Discrete Choice Modeling, Conjoint Analysis

PROJECT OVERVIEW

This groundbreaking research used discrete choice modeling to quantify the relative value of different social media reactions. The study revealed that not all reactions are created equal, with some carrying significantly more emotional weight than others. This work was recognized with the Young ESOMAR Society Award 2019, voted for by nearly 1,000 delegates at the ESOMAR Congress.

KEY FINDINGS

  • One "Love" reaction equals approximately 3.5 "Like" reactions in terms of emotional value
  • A single "Thumbs Down" reaction requires 9.7 "Like" reactions to offset its negative impact
  • The impact of negative reactions diminishes over time, similar to emotional desensitization
  • Context matters: the same reaction can have different emotional impacts based on post content

DEEP DIVE: THE PSYCHOLOGY OF REACTIONS

Comparison of emotional impact: The negative utility of one thumbs down (-9.7) far outweighs the positive utility of one thumbs up (+1)

Diminishing negative impact: The emotional hurt from thumbs down reactions reaches a saturation point

Our research revealed two fascinating psychological phenomena:

  1. Asymmetric Impact: The negative emotional impact of a thumbs down is dramatically stronger than the positive impact of a thumbs up. It takes nearly 10 positive reactions to compensate for a single negative one, demonstrating the profound psychological weight of negative feedback.
  2. Emotional Desensitization: As the number of negative reactions increases, their marginal negative impact decreases. The first thumbs down delivers the strongest emotional blow, but subsequent negative reactions have progressively less impact - similar to how we process repeated emotional stimuli in other aspects of life.

METHODOLOGY

The study employed a conjoint experiment (discrete choice modeling) with 200 US respondents. Participants were presented with varying combinations of thumbs up, thumbs down, and love reactions, and asked to evaluate which combination would make them feel best if received on their social media post.

IMPLICATIONS FOR BRANDS

This research challenges the conventional method of measuring social media engagement by simply summing up reactions. Key implications include:

  • Need for weighted engagement metrics that account for reaction types
  • Importance of considering post context when interpreting negative reactions
  • Value of segmenting reactions by demographics and audience type
  • Potential for creating more nuanced social media success metrics

IMPACT & RECOGNITION

The research was presented at ESOMAR Congress 2019, where it won the Young ESOMAR Society Award through delegate voting. The findings contribute to our understanding of social media engagement and provide a framework for more sophisticated measurement of social media success.
